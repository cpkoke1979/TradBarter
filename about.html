Money is tight. Value isn’t.

Trades are expensive. Quotes are unpredictable. And most people don’t need “a marketplace” — they need one reliable person nearby to sort a job. TradBarter turns skills, time, and practical help into real outcomes by making swapping simple, local, and trustworthy.

A better way to get things done: clear posts, local swaps, and trust that compounds.

The problem (as it really feels)

People are overwhelmed. Household jobs stack up. Costs keep rising. You either pay more than you want, put it off, or rely on favours that aren’t organised. Meanwhile, there’s a huge amount of spare capacity in communities — skills and time that never gets matched to real needs.

  • Small jobs shouldn’t require big cash.
  • Finding help shouldn’t mean trawling chaotic group posts.
  • Trust shouldn’t be a guessing game.

The idea (simple, on purpose)

TradBarter makes swapping normal again — but with structure. You post what you need and what you can give in return. Others browse locally, message within a trade thread, agree the details, and complete the swap.

The wedge: we start with everyday home services and practical help — where value is obvious and demand is constant.

Why we’re starting with one niche

“Do everything for everyone” platforms feel empty and confusing at the start. A focused niche creates density — more relevant posts in one place — which creates outcomes, which creates trust, which creates growth.

High-frequency needs

Everyone has repairs, painting, flat-pack, garden work, cleaning.

Easy to describe

Simple posts beat long explanations and vague requests.

Clear outcomes

Before/after photos + feedback make trust visible fast.

How we keep it from turning into chaos

Barter only scales if people feel safe and the platform stays clean. That’s why TradBarter is built around structure and trust — not endless feeds.

Trade threads

Messages are tied to a specific post. No lost DMs.

Verification

Identity checks and clear profiles reduce bad actors.

Feedback loop

Reputation compounds. The best helpers become obvious.

We start simple, then add stronger safety tools as the community grows (reporting, moderation, stronger verification, dispute support).

What we are / what we’re not

We are

  • Local-first swapping for practical, everyday needs
  • Clear posts and predictable messaging
  • Trust that grows with every completed trade

We’re not

  • A random “everything marketplace” full of noise
  • A chaotic comment-thread experience
  • A place where scams thrive — trust is the product

If this resonates, don’t “sign up because we told you to”. Just see what’s near you first.
